The philosophy of Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) embodies a set of core principles that have shaped its development and adoption worldwide. At its heart, FOSS champions the idea of freedom: the freedom to use, study, modify, and distribute software.





By promoting open access to source code, FOSS empowers users to understand and customize their software to suit their needs. This philosophy encourages collaboration and shared knowledge, fostering a vibrant community of developers and users who contribute to the continual improvement of software.





FOSS should serve the interests of all, guaranteeing user rights, fostering innovation, and promoting social equity.
