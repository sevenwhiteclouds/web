The history of Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) is deeply rooted in the early days of computing. It emerged as a reaction to the closed and proprietary software models prevalent in the industry. In the 1980s, the Free Software movement, led by Richard Stallman, laid the foundation for FOSS by advocating for software users' rights and promoting the concept of copyleft licenses.





The term "Open Source" emerged in the late 1990s, emphasizing the practical benefits of FOSS beyond ideology. Today, FOSS has become a driving force in the software industry, powering critical systems, inspiring innovation, and transforming the way we interact with technology.
